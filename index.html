My Math, Science, Blender, and Coding Videos

Volume of Pyramids
and Cones
An entirely geometric derivation of the volume formulas for pyramids and cones that offers insight to the origin of the 1/3 prefactor. This was also my entry to the Khan Academy Junior Breakthrough Challenge.
Deriving Rotation Matrices
and Python Implementation
The rotation matrices are derived here via rotation of the x and y cartesian coordinates and using trigonometric relations to determine the coordinates of a point in the new rotated x' and y' coordinates.
Nitty Gritty Details
of Image Rotations
Using the rotation matrices derived earlier, I go through the nitty gritty details of writing Python code, without using image processing packages, to rotate an image of my dad and me pixel by pixel.
Geometric Derivation of the
Volume Formula for Spheres
Blender models are constructed here to visualize in three dimensions the correspondence in volume of a hemisphere to the volume between a cone and its enclosing cylinder leading to the volume formula for spheres. This landmark discovery was made by Archimedes in the 3rd century BC.
The Toughest Triangle
Inequality Problem
This is by a long margin the toughest math problem I have encountered in my 14.5 years of existence. It is deceptively simple initially, but bringing together the geometric concepts with the multiple absolute value inequalities was truly a challenge. I worked and wrote equations non-stop in this 23 minute video to solve a single math problem!
Archimedes Principle
Density of a Floating Cylinder
Archimedes Principle states that the buoyant force is equal to the weight of the fluid discplaced. It is used here to derive an expression for the density of a floating cylinder given the density of the fluid, the radius of the log, and the highest point on the cylinder relative to the fluid level.
